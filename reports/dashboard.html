Model performance

A leakage-free churn model: features are taken as of 30 Jun 2025; the label is whether the customer went silent over the next 6 months. Gradient Boosting won at ROC-AUC 0.81.

What drives churn?

Permutation importance (model-agnostic). Recency dominates — how long since a customer's last order is by far the strongest churn signal, exactly as retention theory predicts.

Targeting power — gains by risk decile

Rank customers by predicted risk, split into deciles. The riskiest 10% churn at 86% (1.9× the base rate) — so a retention team working the top deciles reaches real churners, not the whole base.

Risk tiers & revenue exposure

Scoring the active base into risk tiers turns the model into a worklist. High & Critical customers hold $31.6M of historic revenue — the retention priority.

🎯
From prediction to action: a retention campaign targeting only the top-3 risk deciles (~8,500 customers) would reach 76% of predicted churners while contacting just 30% of the base — and if it saves even 10% of the $31.6M at risk, that's ~$3.2M of retained revenue.
